Olivier

Born in 1979, Olivier Rivière discovered the piano, music theory and percussion at the age of 8 years. A native of the island of La Réunion, he entered the Conservatory of la Réunion in 1990 and the Conservatory of Tours in 1997. After that, he obtained a First Prize in Music Teaching, a First Prize in Piano, a First Prize in Chamber Music, a certificate of graduation in Accompaniment and finally followed a comprehensive curriculum graduation in Percussion, Writing and Harmony. Since then, Olivier Rivière has shared his passion between soloist in concerts, accompanist, chamber and teaching of music education. Therefore his musical activities allow him to perform and lead in eclectic repertoires: classical, salsa, jazz, Brassband, Concert Band.

Samuel

A native of Cilaos (island of la Réunion), Samuel started music from childhood, playing maloya rhythms on sheet metal pieces. Expatriate for over 15 years, graduated from the Conservatory of La Rochelle in drums, he is now an accomplished artist. After 20 years of experiences of the stage, he now plays and sings his roots. His music is the product of miscegenation, full of universal colors.

Frédéric

Frédéric Robert, percussionist and foremost conguero, has shared his love of Latin rhythms for twenty years. In the 90s he discovered musical creativity in acid jazz band, funk, disco around the fixed idea of "groove". Rich of experiences as diverse as what can be suggested by human encounters, he never stops to specialize in a universe that he has in his blood: the one of Afro-Cuban rhythms. Today he starts a new aventure with Rolando Salsa & Orquesta and will distil clever tumbaos for aficionados of salsa...

Pierre-Antoine

Pierre-Antoine started the trumpet at the age of 7 years at the Musical Union of Chambray-les-Tours. In 1982, he entered the Tours C.N.R. From 1990, he taught in Charentes Maritime (Cognac, Rochefort, Oléron) and participated in numerous training courses in the region. During this period, he forged his playing style, resolutely oriented toward contemporary music and Latin jazz. Since 2004, he has led the music school Landerneau (Finistère), where he has taught trumpet. Passionate of Latin music, Pierre-Antoine has long played in the salsa band "Tumbaos". He also participated in the project Merzhin Moon Orchestra (2009), a Latin version of the famous western rock band Merzhin.

Amanda

Following a career in opera singing, Amandine Bac has developed her vocal qualities for five years at the conservatory (Tours, Paris, La Rochelle) through private lessons and courses of collective practices in chorus. If her repertoire was more focused on classical music, it is with great enthusiasm that she joined Rolando Salsa & Orquesta to learn the Latin rhythms, that she had first met through dancing.

Louis

Louis began music by the piano. In 1995 he made a training in Jazz at the J.A.M of Montpellier. It is then that he discovered salsa. Fallen in love of this music, he decided to set up a latino group in Charente Maritime. After this experience, he has worked in a company of street theater for 12 years. During this period, he got familiar with the trombone. Currently, Louis studies at the Conservatory of Cognac and Saintes and Poitiers and is happy to make salsa trombone again with Rolando Salsa & Orquesta.

Patrick

After his beginnings as a singer and guitarist, Patrick Manet discovered contrabass, an instrument for which he got passionate. He began self-study of it and did helpful internships with Eddie Gomez, Jean-François Jenny-Clark, Gary Peacock, Hein Van De Geyn. He then studied two years at the Conservatory of Angouleme in the class of Benoît Richard where he perfected his bowing technique by playing notably compositions from the repertoire of the Orchestre de Contrebasses. Composer and arranger graduated from SACEM, he has performed in France and Europe in many formations jazz, chanson, tango and Latin music, and he recorded about twenty albums.

Nicolas

Saxophonist, clarinetist and flutist, he amongst others made musical training with Serge Taynor then studied jazz with Bob Dixon. He graduated in musicology at the University of Poitiers and participated in various stages of improvisation and jazz with Denis Badault, Claude Barthélémy, Laurent Cugny ... He teaches saxophone with musical workshops Syrinx from 1995 to 2002. Eager to meet and curious of all music, he integrated many diverse groups: Trio Pépé (Latin jazz), Charles Bellonzi quintet (hard bop), the Nuits d'Emeraude (show with François Godard storyteller), Noujoum Gnawa (Moroccan music), Sandunga latina (salsa), Michel Delage jazz compagny (big band swing), la famille Zim Boum (fanfare for children) ... Sayag jazz machine (electro-jazz / video concept), with which it has performed in France and worldwide since 2001.
